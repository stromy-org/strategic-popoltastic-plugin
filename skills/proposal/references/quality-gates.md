# Quality Gates — Detailed Checklists

This reference provides comprehensive QA checklists for consulting proposals. Run these checks during Step 6 of the proposal workflow.

These gates align with the Shipley Color Team framework used in professional proposal management:
- **Phase 3 (Strategy approval)** ~ Pink Team — "Is the story right?"
- **Step 6 (Quality gates)** ~ Red Team — "Would the customer score this well?"
- **Pre-submission checklist** ~ Gold Team — "Is it ready to submit?"

## Content Completeness

### Per-Section Checklist

For each section included in the document plan, verify:

- [ ] Section exists in the document with the correct heading
- [ ] Content is substantive (not placeholder, stub, or boilerplate)
- [ ] Section meets its word/page target (if specified)
- [ ] No instances of "TBD", "[placeholder]", "[insert here]", "[TODO]", or lorem ipsum
- [ ] Section addresses its stated purpose (see [sections.md](sections.md))

### Section-Specific Content Checks

**Executive Summary:**
- [ ] Written after all other sections are finalized
- [ ] Contains: client situation, proposed solution, differentiators, proof points, investment summary, next steps
- [ ] Standalone readable — an executive can make a decision from this alone
- [ ] Length: 1-2 pages maximum

**Client Context:**
- [ ] Restates client's situation in original words (not copied from RFP)
- [ ] References specific details from the brief
- [ ] Identifies key challenges and goals

**Objectives:**
- [ ] Objectives are specific and measurable where possible
- [ ] Each objective traces to a deliverable
- [ ] 3-5 primary objectives (not too many)

**Approach & Methodology:**
- [ ] Clear phased structure with rationale
- [ ] Activities connect to stated objectives
- [ ] Client collaboration model described
- [ ] Decision gates included

**Work Plan & Timeline:**
- [ ] Visual format (table or Gantt)
- [ ] Milestones aligned with deliverables
- [ ] Client review points included
- [ ] Dependencies shown
- [ ] Buffer time for reviews

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Each deliverable has: name, description, format, timing
- [ ] Each traces to an objective
- [ ] Format specified (PDF, PPTX, workshop, etc.)

**Team & Qualifications:**
- [ ] Key personnel listed with roles
- [ ] Experience relevant to this engagement highlighted
- [ ] Team structure clear
- [ ] Availability/allocation stated

**Relevant Experience:**
- [ ] 3-5 case studies included
- [ ] Each has: client/industry, challenge, approach, outcome, metrics
- [ ] Relevance to current engagement explained
- [ ] Quantifiable outcomes present

**Pricing & Investment:**
- [ ] Total investment stated
- [ ] Breakdown aligns with approach phases
- [ ] Inclusions and exclusions listed
- [ ] Payment terms specified
- [ ] Assumptions stated

## Claim Substantiation

Every capability claim or differentiator must have supporting evidence. Verify:

- [ ] No unsubstantiated superlatives ("industry-leading", "best-in-class", "world-class") without proof
- [ ] Every "we have experience in X" has a case study or specific reference
- [ ] Metrics include source and timeframe (e.g., "reduced costs by 30% — HealthCo engagement, Q3 2024")
- [ ] Certifications cited with issuing body and current status
- [ ] Client names used only with permission (use industry descriptors otherwise)
- [ ] Team credentials are verifiable and current
- [ ] No claims that contradict other sections

### Evidence Quality Tiers

| Tier | Type | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| 1 (Strongest) | Quantified outcome with named client | "Reduced processing time by 45% for Acme Corp" |
| 2 | Quantified outcome with anonymized client | "Delivered $2.3M savings for a Fortune 500 retailer" |
| 3 | Qualitative outcome with reference | "Client rated engagement 9.2/10 in satisfaction survey" |
| 4 | Credential or certification | "ISO 27001 certified since 2020" |
| 5 (Weakest) | General claim with no reference | "We have deep expertise in healthcare IT" — **needs strengthening** |

**Rule**: No section should rely solely on Tier 5 evidence. Escalate any Tier 5 claims to the team for supporting evidence or remove them.

## Cross-Reference Integrity

- [ ] All "see Section X" references point to existing sections
- [ ] All "see Appendix X" references point to existing appendices
- [ ] Case study IDs referenced in the body exist in the Relevant Experience section
- [ ] Team member names in Approach match those in Team & Qualifications
- [ ] Deliverable names in Timeline match those in Deliverables section
- [ ] Pricing line items correspond to phases in Approach & Methodology
- [ ] Objective numbers referenced elsewhere match the Objectives section

## Section Coherence

Check for internal consistency across the proposal:

### Scope Alignment
- [ ] Approach activities support all stated objectives
- [ ] Deliverables cover all phases in the approach
- [ ] Timeline accommodates all activities in the approach
- [ ] Nothing in the approach is outside the stated scope

### Pricing Alignment
- [ ] Pricing breakdown matches the approach phases
- [ ] Team composition (and rates) can deliver the proposed approach
- [ ] Timeline duration is consistent with the pricing (no free work implied)
- [ ] Optional/additional items are clearly separated

### Team Alignment
- [ ] Team skills match the proposed methodology
- [ ] Team size is realistic for the timeline and scope
- [ ] Key personnel have relevant credentials for their assigned areas
- [ ] No phantom team members (listed in approach but not in team section, or vice versa)

### Timeline Alignment
- [ ] Phase durations are realistic for the described activities
- [ ] Dependencies are respected (no overlaps that don't make sense)
- [ ] Client review periods are included between phases
- [ ] Final delivery date is consistent with all other sections

### Win Theme Consistency
- [ ] 3-5 win themes identified and documented
- [ ] Each theme has supporting evidence (case study, metric, certification)
- [ ] Themes appear in Executive Summary, Approach, Team, and Experience sections
- [ ] No section contradicts a stated win theme
- [ ] Ghost statements (if any) are factual and non-disparaging

## Visual & Formatting Validation

### Document Structure
- [ ] Cover page present with correct client name, project title, date
- [ ] Table of contents is auto-generated (not manually typed)
- [ ] Page numbers in footer
- [ ] Headers contain firm name and/or project title
- [ ] Consistent margins throughout

### Heading Styles
- [ ] All H1 headings use the same style (font, size, color, spacing)
- [ ] All H2 headings use the same style
- [ ] Heading hierarchy is consistent (no H3 without parent H2)
- [ ] No manual formatting used for headings (all via Word styles)

### Tables
- [ ] All tables have header rows
- [ ] Tables don't split across pages in confusing ways
- [ ] Column widths are readable (no truncated content)
- [ ] Consistent table formatting throughout

### Page Layout
- [ ] Page breaks before major sections (H1 level)
- [ ] No orphan headings (heading at bottom of page, content on next)
- [ ] No widow/orphan lines (single line of paragraph on different page)
- [ ] Consistent spacing between sections

### Branding
- [ ] Firm logo appears on cover page (correct version/resolution)
- [ ] Brand colors applied correctly (if using a brand skill)
- [ ] Fonts match brand guidelines
- [ ] No competing visual elements

## Final Pre-Submission Checklist

Run this as the last check before delivery:

### Accuracy
- [ ] Client name spelled correctly throughout
- [ ] Project title consistent on every page
- [ ] Date is correct
- [ ] All numbers add up (especially pricing totals)
- [ ] Contact information is correct and current

### Completeness
- [ ] All sections from the document plan are present
- [ ] No empty sections or placeholder content
- [ ] All referenced appendices exist
- [ ] All cross-references are valid

### Professionalism
- [ ] Spell check passed
- [ ] Grammar check passed
- [ ] No informal language or slang
- [ ] Consistent terminology (same term for the same concept throughout)
- [ ] No internal notes or comments left in the document

### File Hygiene
- [ ] File named according to convention (e.g., `[Firm]_Proposal_[Client]_[Date].docx`)
- [ ] Document properties cleaned (no internal metadata exposed)
- [ ] Track changes resolved (unless redline is intentional)
- [ ] Comments removed (unless review comments are intentional)

## Common Failure Modes

These are the most frequent issues found in proposal reviews. Watch for these especially:

| Failure | Detection | Fix |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| Scope-price mismatch | Pricing doesn't cover all approach phases | Reconcile by adding line items or reducing scope |
| Generic content | Sections could apply to any client | Rewrite with client-specific details and references |
| Missing evidence | Claims without proof points | Add case study references, metrics, or remove claim |
| Inconsistent voice | Sections written by different authors sound different | Harmonize tone, tense, and formality level |
| Timeline fantasy | Unrealistic durations with no buffer | Add review periods, buffer time, realistic estimates |
| Orphan deliverables | Deliverables listed but not connected to approach | Map each deliverable to a phase and objective |
| Credential padding | Irrelevant certifications or experience listed | Keep only credentials relevant to this engagement |
| Missing next steps | Proposal ends without a clear call to action | Add specific next action, contact, and deadline |

---

## Red Flags

If any of these are present, the proposal is NOT client-ready:

1. **Any placeholder text** — "TBD", "[insert]", "[update]", lorem ipsum
2. **Pricing math errors** — line items don't sum to total
3. **Wrong client name** — from a copy-paste of a prior proposal
4. **Contradicting sections** — scope says X, pricing says Y
5. **Missing mandatory sections** — required by RFP but not in the proposal
6. **Unresolved comments or track changes** — internal review artifacts visible
7. **Broken cross-references** — "see Section 5" when Section 5 doesn't exist
8. **No proof points** — claims without any supporting evidence
